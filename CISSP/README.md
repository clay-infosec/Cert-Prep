# CISSP Examination Preparation: A 5-Month Retrospective

The following resources were utilized over a five-month study period. My approach prioritized foundational theory first, followed by technical deep dives, and a final shift toward the "managerial mindset" required for the exam.

---

## 📚 Foundational & Primary Reading

### **(ISC)² CISSP Official Study Guide (OSG), 8th Edition** | 10/10
An essential pillar of any CISSP study plan. While the text is notoriously verbose, the inclusion of real-world scenarios and practical comparisons is invaluable for conceptualizing the material.
> **Note:** I supplemented this with the CCCure May update PDF to ensure alignment with the latest domain changes.

### **11th Hour CISSP (Eric Conrad)** | 8/10
This was my initial entry point into the material. In retrospect, this resource is most effective as a "spark notes" summary during the final two weeks of preparation rather than a primary study source.

---

## 📝 Practice Exams & Knowledge Assessment

### **PocketPrep CISSP Mobile App** | 10/10
* **Metric:** Progressed from a 78% average to 94% after targeted review.

This was my primary tool for gauging readiness in the final month. The ability to perform quick knowledge checks on the go is highly effective for reinforcing "cold" facts and identifying weak domains.

### **Boson CISSP 2021** | 9/10
* **Metric:** Averaged ~80% in "Study Mode."

The technical depth of Boson is unmatched, though candidates should be wary of falling into a "technical rabbit hole." The true value lies in the detailed explanations provided for each answer; I recommend using this to understand the logic of the question rather than memorizing answers.

---

## 🧠 Mindset & Strategic Preparation

### **"How to Think Like a Manager" (Luke Ahmed)** | 9/10
This is a critical resource for pivoting from a technical "fixer" mindset to a risk-based managerial perspective. It is most impactful when read in the final days before the exam to calibrate your decision-making logic.

### **CISSP with Spock & Kirk (YouTube Series)** | 9/10
A thematic but highly effective series for late-stage reinforcement. The unique delivery helps key concepts stick during the final "cramming" phase.

---

## 🤝 Community & Interactive Learning

### **r/cissp & Certification Station (Discord)** | 10/10
Immersing myself in these communities provided essential peer support and exposure to diverse perspectives on exam topics. Engaging with these platforms daily helped maintain momentum throughout the five-month window.

### **Visual Aids: Whiteboard & Note Cards** | 9/10
For complex technical concepts, the act of physical diagramming was essential. Transitioning these concepts from text to a visual format solidified my understanding of intricate security architectures.

---

## 📎 Additional Resources

### **CISSPPrep** | 6/10
While the free questions may offer value for some, the interface and question style did not align well with my specific learning requirements.

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaway for Candidates

> **The CISSP is as much a test of logic and mindset as it is of technical knowledge.** 
> Start with the OSG for depth, use Boson for technical rigor, and finish with Luke Ahmed to ensure you are viewing every question through the lens of risk management.
